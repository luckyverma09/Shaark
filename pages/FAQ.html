FAQ

1. Can GST can be paid through? (Mode of payment)


ANSWER : Pay using Cash/ Cheque/ Demand Draft/UPI within the Challan's validity period Status of the payment will be updated on the GST Portal after confirmation from the Bank.


2. GST can be collected by?


ANSWER : Both Centre and States will simultaneously levy GST across the value chain. Tax will be levied on every supply of goods and services. Centre would levy and collect Central Goods and Services Tax (CGST), and States would levy and collect the State Goods and Services Tax (SGST) on all transactions within a State.


3. GST can be claimed?


ANSWER : You are eligible for a GST refund if you have paid excess tax, exported goods or services, made zero-rated supplies, claimed lower income than presumptive income, or have unutilised input tax credit


4. GST can be collected by which dealer?


ANSWER : As per the GST act, a range of manufacturing and service businesses and traders can register under the GST composition scheme, excluding the following: Individuals or businesses who supply goods through an e-commerce portal operator that collects tax at the source


5. GST for gold?


ANSWER : 3% GST GST Rate on Purchase of Physical Gold GST on gold purchase in India attracts 3% GST (1.5% CGST + 1.5% SGST) rate on the value of gold. So, if the value of gold being purchased is Rs. 10,000 the total GST payable on the transaction will be Rs. 300.

7. GST for food?

ANSWER : GST on food items in India can be 5%, 12%, or 18% based on factors such as the establishment type and location of restaurants or food service providers among others. Goods and services tax has replaced the VAT and Service tax regime on food services.


8. GST for clothes?

ANSWER : Readymade garments of sale value not exceeding Rs. 1000 per piece attract 5% GST. Readymade garments of sale value exceeding Rs. 1000 per piece attract 12% GST.

11. GST is a?

ANSWER : GST is a single tax on the supply of goods and services, right from the manufacturer to the consumer. Credits of input taxes paid at each stage will be available in the subsequent stage of value addition, which makes GST essentially a tax only on value addition at each stage.


12. GST is which type of tax?

ANSWER : Indirect Tax, Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a value-added tax levied on the supply of goods and services for domestic consumption. GST is, therefore, an all-encompassing, single indirect tax law for the entire country. This tax is included in the final price of a product.


13. GST is indirect tax?

ANSWER : In simple words, GST is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services. GST is majorly divided into four types: Central Goods and Service Tax (CGST), State Goods and Service Tax (SGST), Integrated Goods and Services Tax (IGST) and Union Territory Goods and Services Tax (UGST).


14. GST is a direct tax?

ANSWER : Goods and Services Tax (GST): GST is the most common example of indirect tax, which has replaced an array of other indirect taxes in India such as value added tax, service tax, excise duty, purchase tax and more


15. GST is based on the concept of which tax?

ANSWER : GST is a destination-based tax, levied at each stage of the supply chain, from the manufacturer to the consumer.
